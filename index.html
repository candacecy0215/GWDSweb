Reflection

Website: Building a flexbox website has been a valuable practice providing an opportunity to enhance my technical skills. Throughout the process, I explored the knowledge of coding, designing principles and user interactivity. There were some challenges I faced during building the website. One of them was the blank spaces between sections and inflexibility when reducing the screen size. I conducted research online and sought help during the lesson time. With the guidance received, I identified and debugged the issues. Instead of using ‘margin’, I found that using ‘padding’ achieved the desired outcome. Besides, rather than specifying the width and height of each image, setting “max-width”, adjusting the width to 100% and setting the “height” value to auto proved to be more flexible, allowing the image to adapt to the website’s pixel dimensions. Poster: The most challenging aspect of the project, for me, was designing the poster. This presented a practical challenge as this was the first time to design a poster with specific content and concepts that I was not familiar with. I struggle with generating ideas, choosing suitable colours, and determining the optimal placement of the content to give the poster a dynamic appearance, especially creating a sports poster. Applying the elements and knowledge learned in the lectures, I found that using different font size, word colours and font styles would help the creation of a more engaging and informative poster. These design choices helped express the necessary information and highlight the key selling point of the event.
